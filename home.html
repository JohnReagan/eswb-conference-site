The Organization

Engineering Students without Borders (ESWB) is a student-led organization whose mission is to inspire students to apply engineering skills to address community problems, both in local and international communities. We currently have teams working on over 10 projects, which range from the design of a device to help paralyzed patients drink, to the improvement of the water system in communities in the Dominican Republic and Nicaragua. Although we have "engineering" in our name, several of our teams have non-engineering student members, and we welcome anyone with an interest in the intersection of technology and community development to attend our conference. ***too long?***

FAQ

How will this conference benefit me?

You will be able to meet students from across the east coast who are working on humanitarian technology projects and learn from them and other community development experts through workshops, a design challenge, and a poster session. Through conversations with other student leaders, you can also find new ideas for running EWB at UMBC.

How much does attending cost?

There is only a $15 registration fee per student, which helps cover the cost of meals.

Who can I contact for more questions?

We'd love to answer your questions! Contact our Vice President, Alisha, at amg4ft@virginia.edu.

Is there lodging available for me?

We are working to find student hosts to provide free lodging to all conference attendees. That said, if you are able to get funding for a hotel, please help us reduce the number of student hosts we need to find.
